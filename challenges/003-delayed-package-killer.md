**Status:** Upcoming

## The Challenge

Pick one Amazon item that's now "delayed due to shipping crisis" and redesign it for local 3D printing or hand tools. Post before/after.

## Rules

1. Screenshot an Amazon item showing a shipping delay (we've all got them)
2. Recreate it -- 3D print it, fabricate it, or build it from hardware store parts
3. Post before/after in #challenge-3 with **#DelayedPackageKiller**
4. Include: what the Amazon item was, what you built, and how much you saved

## Ideas

- **Phone mount** -- Amazon says 3 weeks? Print one in 2 hours.
- **Shelf bracket** -- bend one from scrap metal or cut from plywood.
- **Cable organizer** -- print it or make from binder clips.
- **Replacement knob** -- stove, dresser, whatever. Measure, model, print.
- **Tool holder** -- pegboard hooks from wire hangers.
- **Kid's toy** -- wooden toy from scrap lumber beats plastic from Shenzhen.
- **Garden stake** -- cut from old blinds or PVC pipe.

## No 3D Printer?

No problem. Most of these can be made with:
- A hand saw + sandpaper
- Cardboard + hot glue
- Wire hangers + pliers
- PVC pipe + a heat gun (or boiling water)

The point isn't perfection. The point is independence.

## Bonus Points

- Share your STL file so others can print it
- Cost comparison (Amazon price vs your build cost)
- Make it BETTER than the Amazon version
