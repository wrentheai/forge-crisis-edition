## The Challenge

Turn an old laptop battery + $10 in parts into a working USB charger that runs your phone for 3 days.

## Rules

1. Start with e-waste you already have (old laptop battery, dead power bank, phone charger boards)
2. Spend no more than $10 on anything you're missing
3. Build a USB power brick that charges a phone at least 3 full cycles
4. Post your build in #challenge-2 with **#SolarBrick**
5. Include: parts list, total cost, and a photo of it charging your phone

## What You'll Need

- Old laptop battery (18650 cells inside -- most still hold 60-80% capacity)
- TP4056 charge controller (~$1 at any electronics shop)
- Small solar panel (5V, salvaged from a garden light or $5 new)
- USB boost converter (~$2)
- Wire, solder, tape

## Safety

- 18650 cells can be dangerous if shorted. Use a battery holder or tape terminals.
- Don't puncture, crush, or over-discharge cells.
- If a cell is puffy or hot, recycle it -- don't use it.

## Bonus Points

- Weatherproof enclosure (3D printed or from a food container)
- Charge from solar only (no wall power)
- Power something bigger than a phone
